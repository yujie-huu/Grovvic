## Plant Health

### Why are my leaves turning yellow?
1. Check watering – reduce if soil is too wet.
2. Improve soil drainage if water is pooling.
3. Test for nutrient deficiency, especially nitrogen.
4. Add compost or organic fertilizer if needed.

### How can I treat powdery mildew naturally?
1. Improve air circulation by pruning crowded plants.
2. Avoid overhead watering.
3. Spray leaves with a mix of baking soda and water.
4. Use neem oil as an organic fungicide.

### What causes root rot and how do I prevent it?
1. Avoid overwatering and soggy soil.
2. Plant in well-drained soil or raised beds.
3. Remove infected roots and replant in fresh soil.
4. Choose resistant plant varieties.

### How do I deal with aphids or caterpillars?
1. Spray leaves with water to knock pests off.
2. Introduce natural predators like ladybugs.
3. Use neem oil or insecticidal soap.
4. Remove heavily infested leaves.

### What should I do if my plant has brown spots on the leaves?
1. Remove infected leaves.
2. Improve airflow between plants.
3. Avoid watering leaves directly.
4. Apply an organic fungicide if needed.


## Soil and Nutrients

### How to reuse spent compost?
1. Add organic matter and use it again for planting.
2. Improve your garden soil by digging-in spent compost.
3. Mulch your beds and borders to lock-in moisture and suppress weeds.
4. Improve the appearance and health of lawns.
5. Boost an existing compost bin or start a new one.

### How to mulch with organic matter?
1. Mulch in late winter or early spring.
2. Calculate how much mulch you’ll need.
3. Choose your mulching material.
4. Prepare the ground for mulching.

### How do I know if my soil is lacking nutrients?
1. Look for signs like pale leaves or poor growth.
2. Test soil with a home kit or lab service.
3. Add compost or organic matter to improve fertility.
4. Apply missing nutrients in balanced, organic form.

### What is the best way to improve clay or sandy soils?
1. Mix in compost or well-rotted manure.
2. Add organic mulch to retain moisture.
3. Avoid compaction by not walking on wet soil.
4. Grow cover crops to build soil health.

### How do I make and use compost effectively?
1. Collect kitchen scraps and garden waste.
2. Mix green (wet) and brown (dry) materials.
3. Turn the compost regularly for aeration.
4. Use finished compost when dark and crumbly.

### Why is soil pH important and how can I adjust it?
1. Test pH with a kit (most plants prefer 6–7).
2. Add lime to raise acidic soil.
3. Add sulfur or composted leaves to lower alkaline soil.
4. Retest soil after a few weeks.

### What are organic alternatives to chemical fertilizers?
1. Use compost or worm castings.
2. Apply seaweed or fish emulsion.
3. Add rock dust for minerals.
4. Grow nitrogen-fixing plants like beans.


## Watering and Irrigation

### How do I know when my plants need watering?
1. Check soil moisture with your finger.
2. Water when the top 2–3 cm of soil is dry.
3. Water deeply rather than lightly.
4. Adjust frequency depending on weather.

### What are some tips to avoid overwatering?
1. Use pots with drainage holes.
2. Water only when soil feels dry.
3. Mulch soil to reduce evaporation.
4. Group plants with similar water needs.

### What is drip irrigation and why is it water-efficient?
1. Drip irrigation delivers water directly to roots.
2. It reduces evaporation and water waste.
3. It prevents wet leaves, lowering disease risk.
4. It saves time compared to hand watering.

### How can I use mulch to reduce water loss?
1. Spread organic mulch around plants.
2. Keep mulch 5 cm away from stems.
3. Apply mulch 5–8 cm thick.
4. Refresh mulch yearly.


## Sustainable Practices

### How can I reduce or avoid chemical pesticide use?
1. Choose resistant plant varieties.
2. Encourage natural predators like birds.
3. Use traps or barriers for pests.
4. Apply organic sprays only when needed.

### What is companion planting and how does it help?
1. Pair plants that support each other’s growth.
2. Grow marigolds to deter pests.
3. Plant basil with tomatoes to improve flavor.
4. Mix flowers with vegetables to attract pollinators.

### How do I attract pollinators and beneficial insects?
1. Plant native flowers rich in nectar.
2. Avoid chemical sprays.
3. Provide water sources like shallow dishes.
4. Grow a variety of flowering plants year-round.

### What are good native or drought-resistant plants for Victoria?
1. Kangaroo paw.
2. Lomandra.
3. Correa (native fuchsia).
4. Eucalyptus species (small varieties).


## Seasonal and Climate Adaptation

### When is the best time to sow or transplant vegetables?
1. Check the seed packet for timing.
2. Plant cool-season crops in autumn/winter.
3. Plant warm-season crops in spring/summer.
4. Harden off seedlings before transplanting.

### How do I protect plants from frost or heatwaves?
1. Cover plants with frost cloth or shade cloth.
2. Mulch soil to regulate temperature.
3. Water early in the morning during heat.
4. Move potted plants to sheltered areas.
